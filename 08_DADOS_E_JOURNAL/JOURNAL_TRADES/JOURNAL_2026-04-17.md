  None of the three SIMULATE trades hit their stop loss. All three exited before their time stop
  or at a disciplined target. This is the intended behavior: the pipeline identifies entries with
  positive expected value, the exit rules capture gains before the thesis expires. The framework
  is operating as designed. Time stops are not a failure mode — they are a capital recycling
  mechanism. But proactive exits near target (like PT003) are superior to passive time stop waits.

  The first CONFIRMED breakout in the Breakout Intelligence Layer was logged today: WLD LONG at

  Two conditions converged at PT003 exit: RSI overbought (74.91) and AT_WEEKLY_HIGH structure.
  Adding HIGH news severity (crypto hacks) to that picture eliminates any reason to hold through

  PT003's score degraded from 27/35 at entry to 24/35 today — a 3-point drop. Yet the trade
  returned +1.8R. Score degradation during a winning trade reflects the Score Engine correctly
  adjusting as the Potential component diminishes when price approaches the resistance target.
  Score is not a hold signal — it is an entry quality signal. Do not confuse score degradation
  on a winning trade as a reason to exit early. The exit rules (RSI, time stop, target proximity)
  are the correct exit drivers, not the score trend once in a position.
