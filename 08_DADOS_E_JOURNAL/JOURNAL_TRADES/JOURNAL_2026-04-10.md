DATE: 2026-04-10
PHASE: SIMULATE (Day 2)

LESSON_1 — HOLD THROUGH DAY 1 DRAWDOWN WAS CORRECT:
  Day 1 closed at -$0.24 with AVAX at 9.05 ($0.02 above the 7D_AVG invalidation).
  The emotional pressure was to cut early. The plan said: stop is 8.501, no action
  unless that level is breached or 7D_AVG closes below 9.03. Neither happened.
  Day 2 AVAX prints 9.38 — the thesis was intact, the plan was right, the emotion
  was noise. This is exactly what Lesson 1 from Day 1 was preparing for.
  Rule confirmed: check the stop, not the emotion.

LESSON_2 — NEAR'S RELATIVE STRENGTH WAS PREDICTIVE:
  Day 1 lesson identified NEAR's +2.60% vs BTC -1.02% as a real signal.
  Day 2 confirms: NEAR advanced further to 1.37 while BTC was still recovering
  from its retracement. Assets that hold (or gain) during broad market weakness
  tend to outperform on the recovery. The original thesis (highest 7D deviation,
  SETUP structure) plus the Day 1 relative strength signal combine into a higher
  conviction hold. This validates using relative strength as a forward indicator.

LESSON_3 — THE STOP-LOSS IS A RULE, NOT A SUGGESTION (TAO LESSON):
  TAO -18.11% in a single session. Any position held without a hard stop in this
  environment would have been catastrophic. The Euru OS ATR×1.5 stop methodology
  exists precisely because gaps like this are real and frequent in crypto.
  The 1% max risk rule means even a direct stop-hit is a controlled, survivable
  loss — not an account-level event. TAO is not in the portfolio because it did
  not pass the pipeline. The system worked by exclusion.
  Rule reinforced: stops are set at entry based on ATR, never moved down, never
  ignored. No exceptions. No "let me see one more candle."

LESSON_4 — ARB CANDIDATE REQUIRES PATIENCE BEFORE PT003:
  ARB printed +9.71% on the day — a strong move that could trigger FOMO entry.
  The correct response is: flag it, monitor it, run it through the pipeline on
  the next morning scan. A 9.71% day does NOT mean entry. It means the asset is
  on the radar. Score 26/35 is promising but the scout must confirm SETUP state
  on the daily TF with proper structure and volume alignment before any entry is
  considered. Chasing a move the day it happens violates every entry rule in the
  playbook. The signal is the scan, not the price action you missed.

LESSON_5 — IMPROVING NEWS ENVIRONMENT CHANGES THE RISK CALCULUS:
  News severity at MEDIUM (vs HIGH on Day 1) is not just a data point — it
  expands the set of viable entry signals. Under HIGH severity, any new position
  requires elevated scrutiny. Under MEDIUM, the pipeline operates closer to
  standard. If ARB confirms as PT003, the news context will be materially more
  favorable than when PT001/PT002 were entered. Factor this into the confidence
  score — not as a reason to be reckless, but as a proper input to the assessment.
