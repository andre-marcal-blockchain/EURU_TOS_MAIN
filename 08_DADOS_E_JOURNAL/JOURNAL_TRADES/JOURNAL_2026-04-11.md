## Lessons Learned — Day 3 of SIMULATE

```
DATE: 2026-04-11
PHASE: SIMULATE (Day 3)

LESSON_1 — SCORE AT ENTRY IS NOT SCORE AT HOLD:
  PT001 entered at 25/35 — now 18/35. PT002 entered at 25/35 — now 17/35. In both cases,
  the score has declined significantly while the price has either marginally advanced or stalled.
  The score is a snapshot of conditions at the moment of the scan — not a guarantee of sustained
  quality. A position that scores BOA at entry can drop to MEDIA within days if volume contracts,
  trend shifts to MIXED, or relative strength fades. Lesson: track score trajectory across days,
  not just at entry. A falling score on an open position is an early warning signal even if the
  stop has not been hit. Consider adding score trend to the daily review criteria.

LESSON_2 — THE TWO-DAY PROTOCOL WORKED AS DESIGNED (ARB):
  ARB was identified on Day 2 with Score 26/35. The plan said: flag it, don't enter, run the
  morning scan tomorrow. Day 3 scan confirmed Score 27/35, SETUP state still intact, and news
  dropped to LOW — better conditions than Day 2. By waiting one session, the entry was made
  under improved news context (LOW vs MEDIUM) with a higher score. The discipline of the
  identification-to-entry protocol is validated again. Discipline is not a cost — it is an
  advantage. Every trade that followed the protocol has had a documented, plan-driven rationale.

LESSON_3 — RSI AT ENTRY MATTERS: THREE POSITIONS, THREE RSI LEVELS:
  PT001 entry RSI: 50.62 — neutral, room to run.
  PT002 entry RSI: 61.57 — elevated, still room.
  PT003 entry RSI: 68.05 — high, thin margin before overbought.
  The pattern is clear: each subsequent entry has had a higher RSI at the point of entry. This
  correlates with entering later into momentum moves. Ideal entry RSI is 50–65 on the daily TF.
  Above 65, the position is entering a momentum phase that may be closer to exhaustion. RSI above
  70 is a known overbought zone in crypto. For future entries, add an explicit RSI gate: if RSI
  at entry > 65, require extra confirmation (e.g., a pullback to the 7D_AVG or a lower-timeframe
  retest) before committing. The plan mitigates this with the 50% securing and trailing stop rules,
  but prevention is better than mitigation.

LESSON_4 — NEAR IS TEACHING PATIENCE AND THE VALUE OF THE TIME STOP:
  NEAR has been flat at 1.34 for 3 days. It has not gone up. It has not gone down. It is
  consolidating at the entry level. In a discretionary system, this is where traders often close
  "because nothing is happening." In a systematic system, nothing happening is not a reason to
  exit — the stop level and time stop are the reasons to exit. NEAR is still above its stop, above
  its 7D_AVG, with OBV RISING and MACD BULLISH. The thesis has not been invalidated. The time
  stop (2026-04-15, 4 days) is the systematic answer to the question "when do we give up?" Not
  before then. This lesson reinforces why the time stop was built into the framework.

LESSON_5 — NEWS LOW IS THE CLEANEST ENTRY ENVIRONMENT: USE IT FULLY:
  The first LOW-severity news session in the SIMULATE phase coincided with the ARB entry. This
  is not a coincidence — the lower the news risk, the more confidence the pipeline signals carry.
  Under HIGH news (Days 1–2), every signal had to be weighed against the macro headwind. Under
  LOW news, the signal is what it is — uncontaminated by external shock risk. Future planning:
  when news is LOW, pipeline signals that might be borderline under HIGH should be treated with
  higher weight. When news is HIGH or CRITICAL, raise the threshold for new entries. The news
  severity tier is a first-order input to entry quality, not a footnote.

LESSON_6 — THREE POSITIONS REQUIRE ACTIVE PORTFOLIO-LEVEL THINKING:
  With one position (PT001), the only question is: is the thesis intact? With two (PT001 + PT002),
  the question expands: are the two correlated, and does combined risk stay within limits? With
  three (PT001 + PT002 + PT003), the portfolio layer becomes a distinct management task. Today,
  33.88% of capital is deployed across three assets in different states: one advancing slowly (AVAX),
  one stalling (NEAR), one just entered (ARB). Each requires separate monitoring criteria but the
  portfolio as a whole requires synthesis: if two of three stall, what is the time cost of capital?
  If all three stop-hit simultaneously (3% loss), is that within tolerance? The answers are yes
  (within limits), but the question must be asked explicitly. Portfolio management is not just the
  sum of individual trade management — it is a separate discipline.
```
